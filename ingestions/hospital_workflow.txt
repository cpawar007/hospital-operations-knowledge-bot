----------------------------------------
HOSPITAL INTERNAL WORKFLOWS
----------------------------------------

This document explains how different hospital operations function internally, including ICU, surgery, diagnostics, pharmacy, and patient handling workflows.

----------------------------------------
1. OUTPATIENT WORKFLOW (OPD)
----------------------------------------
- Patient visits hospital
- Registers at reception
- Gets consultation with doctor
- Doctor may:
  - Prescribe medication
  - Recommend tests
  - Suggest admission

----------------------------------------
2. INPATIENT WORKFLOW (IPD)
----------------------------------------
- Patient is admitted
- Bed/room allocated
- Daily monitoring by nurses
- Doctor visits regularly
- Treatment continues until recovery
- Discharge process initiated after stability

----------------------------------------
3. ICU WORKFLOW
----------------------------------------
ICU is for critically ill patients.

Process:
- Patient shifted to ICU immediately if critical
- Connected to monitoring systems:
  - Heart rate monitor
  - Oxygen levels
  - Blood pressure
- Life-support may include:
  - Ventilator
  - IV medications
- Continuous observation (24/7)
- Limited visitor access

----------------------------------------
4. SURGERY WORKFLOW
----------------------------------------
STEP 1: PRE-SURGERY
- Doctor recommends surgery
- Patient evaluation and tests
- Consent form signed
- Operation scheduled

STEP 2: DURING SURGERY
- Patient moved to Operation Theatre (OT)
- Anesthesia administered
- Surgery performed by surgeon

STEP 3: POST-SURGERY
- Patient shifted to recovery room
- Monitoring for complications
- Then moved to ward or ICU

----------------------------------------
5. DIAGNOSTIC WORKFLOW
----------------------------------------
- Doctor prescribes test
- Patient goes to lab/radiology
- Tests include:
  - Blood tests
  - X-ray
  - CT scan
  - MRI
- Reports generated
- Results sent to doctor for review

----------------------------------------
6. PHARMACY WORKFLOW
----------------------------------------
- Doctor prescribes medicines
- Patient visits hospital pharmacy
- Pharmacist verifies prescription
- Medicines dispensed
- Usage instructions provided

----------------------------------------
7. EMERGENCY WORKFLOW
----------------------------------------
- Patient arrives without appointment
- Immediate triage (priority check)
- Stabilization begins
- Doctor takes quick decision:
  - Treat and discharge
  - Admit patient
  - Shift to ICU

----------------------------------------
8. BILLING WORKFLOW
----------------------------------------
- Charges added for:
  - Consultation
  - Room
  - Tests
  - Medicines
  - Procedures
- Bill updated continuously
- Final bill generated at discharge

----------------------------------------
9. FOLLOW-UP WORKFLOW
----------------------------------------
- Doctor recommends revisit
- Patient books appointment
- Carries reports and prescriptions
- Further treatment planned

----------------------------------------
10. INTER-DEPARTMENT FLOW
----------------------------------------
Patients may move between departments:

- OPD → Diagnostics → OPD
- OPD → Admission → Ward
- Ward → ICU (if condition worsens)
- ICU → Ward (if improves)
- Ward → Discharge

----------------------------------------
IMPORTANT RULES
----------------------------------------
- Doctor decision drives all workflows
- Critical cases are prioritized
- ICU is only for severe conditions
- Tests must be reviewed before treatment changes
- Billing is updated at every stage

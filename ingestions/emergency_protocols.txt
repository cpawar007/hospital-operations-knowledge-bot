----------------------------------------
EMERGENCY & CRITICAL CARE PROTOCOLS
----------------------------------------

Emergency services are available 24/7 and are designed to handle life-threatening conditions requiring immediate medical attention.

----------------------------------------
WHAT IS AN EMERGENCY
----------------------------------------
A medical emergency is any condition that:
- Threatens life
- Causes severe pain or distress
- Requires immediate intervention

----------------------------------------
COMMON EMERGENCY CASES
----------------------------------------
- Chest pain / suspected heart attack
- Severe breathing difficulty
- Unconsciousness
- Stroke symptoms (weakness, slurred speech)
- Severe bleeding
- Accidents / trauma
- Seizures
- High fever with complications
- Severe allergic reactions

----------------------------------------
EMERGENCY FLOW (STEP-BY-STEP)
----------------------------------------

STEP 1: PATIENT ARRIVAL
- Patient arrives via:
  - Walk-in
  - Ambulance

STEP 2: TRIAGE PROCESS
- Patient condition is assessed immediately
- Priority levels assigned:

  RED (Critical)
  - Immediate life-saving treatment
  - No waiting

  YELLOW (Moderate)
  - Urgent but stable
  - Short waiting time

  GREEN (Minor)
  - Non-critical
  - Can wait

STEP 3: INITIAL STABILIZATION
- Vital signs checked
- Oxygen support if needed
- IV fluids started
- Emergency medications given

STEP 4: DOCTOR INTERVENTION
- Emergency doctor evaluates patient
- Decision taken:
  - Treat and discharge
  - Admit patient
  - Shift to ICU
  - Refer to specialist

STEP 5: DIAGNOSTIC SUPPORT
- Immediate tests:
  - Blood tests
  - ECG
  - X-ray / CT scan

STEP 6: FURTHER ACTION
- Surgery (if required)
- ICU transfer (if critical)
- Ward admission (if stable)

----------------------------------------
ICU PROTOCOL
----------------------------------------
- Continuous monitoring (24/7)
- Life-support systems:
  - Ventilator
  - Cardiac monitor
- Strict infection control
- Limited visitor access

----------------------------------------
AMBULANCE SERVICE
----------------------------------------
- Available 24/7
- Equipped with:
  - Oxygen support
  - Emergency medicines
  - Trained staff

----------------------------------------
IMPORTANT RULES
----------------------------------------
- Emergency cases are treated FIRST
- No prior appointment needed
- Payment can be done AFTER stabilization
- Critical patients are directly moved to ICU

----------------------------------------
FIRST RESPONSE ADVICE (BOT USE)
----------------------------------------
If user describes emergency symptoms:
- Stay calm and reassure
- Suggest immediate hospital visit
- Recommend calling ambulance if severe
- Do NOT delay response

----------------------------------------
BOT RESPONSE LOGIC
----------------------------------------
If user says:
- "I have chest pain"
→ Treat as emergency → suggest immediate hospital visit

- "Someone fainted"
→ Suggest emergency care + basic first aid

- "Accident happened"
→ Suggest ambulance immediately

- "Breathing problem"
→ Treat as urgent → recommend emergency department
